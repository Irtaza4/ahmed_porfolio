01 — The Challenge

A real service with
no search credibility

AWZ Auto Transport was providing genuine vehicle shipping services but competing in a search landscape where domain authority decides who wins. Rivals with stronger off-page profiles were dominating every high-intent query — "car transport," "auto shipping quotes," "vehicle transport near me." Without a robust external trust signal framework, no amount of on-site optimization would shift rankings. The task was clear: build the off-page foundation that Google uses to determine who deserves page one.

01

An Authority Score of 9 isn't enough to compete for transactional terms. A major inbound link injection from credible, relevant sources was the first priority.

03

Zero AI Presence

02 — How It Was Done

3 phases. 90 days.
One authority engine built.

Off-page SEO isn't a volume game — it's about earning the right links, from the right sources, in the right sequence. The campaign was structured as three compounding phases, each building momentum for the next.

PHASE ONE Profile Audit & Cleanup

Fix the Foundation Before You Build on It

Before a single new link was acquired, the existing profile was audited end-to-end. Toxic and spammy backlinks were identified, flagged, and disavowed to clear any penalty risk. NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency was enforced across all existing citations to strengthen local and entity signals. Anchor text distribution was analyzed for over-optimization that could trigger manual actions.

With a clean baseline locked in, first-wave outreach was structured around niche-relevant automotive and logistics directories — planting the first 20–30 credible referring domains fast and establishing topical relevance from day one.

Toxic Link Disavow Anchor Text Audit NAP Consistency Auto Transport Directories Logistics Citations Local Business Listings Profile Baseline Set
PHASE TWO Authority Link Acquisition

Earning Real Backlinks from Trusted Sources

The core of the campaign. A multi-channel outreach strategy targeted automotive blogs, vehicle relocation guides, moving and relocation platforms, and transport industry publications. Guest posting placed contextually rich content with natural, editorially integrated backlinks — no link farms, no PBNs, no shortcuts of any kind.

HARO pitches were submitted for transport and logistics editorial slots, securing high-authority media placements. A competitor link gap analysis identified the highest-value sites that rival auto transport companies held — and systematic outreach earned those same placements. By month 2, referring domains had passed 80 and were climbing hard toward 133.

Guest Posting — Auto & Logistics HARO Media Outreach Competitor Link Gap Analysis Editorial Placements Moving & Relocation Platforms Contextual Anchor Strategy Tiered Link Structure Digital PR Pitches
PHASE THREE Velocity Push & AI Signals

Crossing 10K Links & Entering AI Search

Month 3 focused on two objectives: hitting the 10,000 backlink milestone to establish a dominant volume signal, and laying the groundwork for AI search visibility. Brand entity signals were reinforced across the web so AI systems — ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, Gemini — could confidently associate the domain with auto transport topics.

The strategy worked: Google AI Mode began citing 7 pages from the domain, delivering an AI Visibility Score of 14 from an absolute starting point of zero. Most competitors in this space aren't building AI presence yet — which means a 90-day head start compounds significantly as AI search continues to grow.
