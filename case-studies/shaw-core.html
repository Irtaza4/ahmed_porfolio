01 — The Challenge

A skilled contractor no one could find

ShawCore Remodeling had the craftsmanship and the reviews to succeed — but the website was invisible. In a market where homeowners search before they ever pick up the phone, not showing up meant losing to less-capable competitors who simply invested in their online presence. The goal wasn't vanity rankings — it was generating a steady, organic pipeline of qualified local leads.

01
No Local SEO Foundation
Google Business Profile was unclaimed or underoptimized. Local citation signals were inconsistent, and the site had no geo-targeted content to surface in map pack or local organic results.
02
Thin Service Pages
Each service page contained minimal content with no keyword strategy, no semantic depth, and no schema markup — making them near-impossible to rank for high-intent queries like "kitchen remodeling near me."
03
Weak Link Profile
Fewer than 40 referring domains with almost no editorial or industry-specific links. The domain carried insufficient authority to compete against established local remodeling companies.
04
Zero AI Search Presence
The contractor had no visibility in AI-generated answers — a channel that homeowners are rapidly adopting for project discovery and contractor vetting questions.
02 — How It Was Done

4 phases. 5 disciplines.
6 months of compounding results.

Home services SEO is different. It demands a hyper-local approach, trust-signal architecture, and content that converts intent into contact. This engagement was built around those realities — each phase layered on the last, each discipline amplifying the others. Here's precisely what was executed, when, and what it delivered.

PHASE ONE Technical & Local Foundation

Fixing the Site & Owning the Map Pack

Before any content or link work, two urgent foundations had to be set simultaneously: a technically clean website and an optimized local presence. A full technical audit surfaced crawl errors, slow load times, missing canonical tags, and unstructured schema — all actively suppressing rankings. In parallel, the Google Business Profile was claimed, fully built out, and optimized with geo-relevant categories, service areas, photo optimization, and Q&A seeding.

NAP (Name, Address, Phone) data was audited across 50+ directories and corrected for consistency — a critical local ranking signal that had been ignored entirely. LocalBusiness and Service schema were implemented on all key pages. By the end of month 2, the site was technically sound and the local entity was fully established in Google's eyes.

Technical Audit & Cleanup Core Web Vitals — Improved Google Business Profile Build NAP Consistency — 50+ Directories LocalBusiness Schema Canonical Tag Implementation XML Sitemap Rebuild Crawl Error Resolution
PHASE TWO On-Page & Service Page SEO

Turning Every Service Page into a Ranking Asset

With a stable foundation, the focus moved to rebuilding every service and location page from scratch. Over 800 targeted keywords were researched and mapped across service types (kitchen remodeling, bathroom renovation, basement finishing, additions) and geographic modifiers. Each page was structured for transactional intent — the kind of searcher who is ready to request a quote, not just browse.

Title tags, meta descriptions, H-tag hierarchies, and body copy were rebuilt with dual purpose: satisfying search intent and building conversion trust. E-E-A-T signals were embedded — contractor credentials, licensing mentions, project portfolio references, and review integration. Image alt text, filename conventions, and compression were standardized sitewide. By month 4, service pages were emerging in local organic results for key phrases.

800+ Keywords Researched Intent-Mapped Page Rewrites Service Page Architecture Location Landing Pages E-E-A-T Signal Integration Title & Meta Rebuild Image SEO Review Schema Markup
PHASE THREE Content Authority & Local Topical Coverage

Building the Content Moat Competitors Won't Replicate

The highest-leverage phase was constructing a content architecture that positioned ShawCore as the local expert — not just a contractor with a website. A content model was developed around homeowner questions: cost guides, project timelines, material comparisons, permit requirements, and contractor selection criteria. These topics were mapped into tight content clusters aligned with the service pages, creating an internal link network that funneled authority to the pages that convert.

FAQ content with structured schema was deployed to capture featured snippets and AI-cited answer blocks. Local-specific content covered city-level project considerations and regional permitting nuances — signals that reinforce geographic relevance to both Google and AI platforms. By month 5, topical coverage was deep enough that the domain began ranking for a wide range of informational queries feeding the conversion funnel.

Content Cluster Architecture 30+ Articles Published FAQ Schema — 120+ Queries Cost & Project Guides Local Geographic Content Entity Reinforcement Internal Link Architecture Featured Snippet Targeting
PHASE FOUR Off-Page SEO & AI Search Optimization

Building Authority & Earning AI Citations — Simultaneously

The final phase ran two high-impact campaigns in parallel. The off-page push focused on building a legitimate link profile through home improvement editorial placements, local business association mentions, contractor directory listings, and supplier/brand partnerships. 576 unique referring domains were secured — a substantial profile for a local contractor in a 6-month window. No link farms, no shortcuts: every placement was manually evaluated for relevance and editorial quality.

Running alongside the link campaign was a structured push for AI search visibility. Content was reformatted for citability — clear answer blocks, entity-rich structured data, and LLM-friendly formatting that makes it easy for AI platforms to surface and attribute the contractor. The results: an AI Visibility Score of 24 achieved from zero, with ShawCore now appearing in ChatGPT (4 mentions, 10 cited pages), Google AI Overview (7 mentions, 5 cited pages), Google AI Mode (9 mentions, 1 cited page), and Gemini (8 mentions, 2 cited pages).
